# Sample STAR Score Reports (Synthetic Examples)

> **NOTE: Every student, score, and detail below is INVENTED for a demonstration project. These are not real children and not real results. They exist to show how to read a report. Your child's actual report may look different and should be interpreted with their teacher.**

These four examples show different profiles parents commonly see. Each includes the numbers and a plain-language interpretation.

---

## Sample Report A — Maya R., Grade 4, STAR Reading (Winter window)

| Score | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Scaled Score (SS) | 642 |
| Percentile Rank (PR) | 82 |
| Grade Equivalent (GE) | 6.1 |
| Student Growth Percentile (SGP) | 71 |
| Performance Level | At/Above Benchmark |

**How to read this:** Maya is performing well above the typical fourth-grade level. Her PR of 82 means she scored as well as or better than 82% of fourth-graders nationally. Her GE of 6.1 does **not** mean she should be doing sixth-grade work — it means her reading performance resembles that of an average student early in sixth grade. Her SGP of 71 shows she also grew more than most peers who started where she did, so she is not just high — she is still climbing. This is a strong, healthy profile.

## Sample Report B — Daniel K., Grade 6, STAR Reading (Winter window)

| Score | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Scaled Score (SS) | 588 |
| Percentile Rank (PR) | 31 |
| Grade Equivalent (GE) | 4.8 |
| Student Growth Percentile (SGP) | 58 |
| Performance Level | On Watch |

**How to read this:** Daniel is a little below the benchmark but not far off. His PR of 31 places him below the common "benchmark met" line of 40, which is why he shows as "On Watch" rather than "At/Above Benchmark." However — and this is the important part — his SGP of 58 means he is growing **faster than more than half** of students who started at a similar level. So while he has not yet reached grade-level expectations, he is moving in the right direction. The school will likely monitor him rather than place him in formal intervention. This is the kind of "mixed" report that is easy to misread if you only look at the percentile.

## Sample Report C — Sofia M., Grade 3, STAR Reading (Winter window)

| Score | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Scaled Score (SS) | 412 |
| Percentile Rank (PR) | 8 |
| Grade Equivalent (GE) | 1.9 |
| Student Growth Percentile (SGP) | 22 |
| Performance Level | Urgent Intervention |

**How to read this:** Sofia is performing well below grade-level expectations. A PR of 8 means she scored higher than about 8% of third-graders nationally, and her SGP of 22 means her growth has also been slower than most similar peers. The "Urgent Intervention" label signals that she would benefit from intensive, focused reading support. This is not a verdict on Sofia's ability or potential — it is a signal that she needs more help now. A productive next step is a conversation with her teacher about what support the school can put in place and what would help at home.

## Sample Report D — Aiden T., Grade 5, STAR Math (Winter window)

| Score | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Scaled Score (SS) | 795 |
| Percentile Rank (PR) | 54 |
| Grade Equivalent (GE) | 5.6 |
| Student Growth Percentile (SGP) | 49 |
| Performance Level | At/Above Benchmark |

**How to read this:** Aiden is right around the middle. His PR of 54 means he scored a little above the national average for fifth-graders in math, and his performance level is "At/Above Benchmark" because he is past the common PR-40 line. His SGP of 49 means his growth is about average — roughly in the middle of peers who started where he did. This is a solid, on-track profile with no red flags. A reasonable goal would be steady growth into the next window.

---

## A note on reading these together

Notice that the percentile alone can mislead. Daniel (B) looks "behind" on percentile but is growing well. Sofia (C) is behind on both achievement and growth, which is why her report calls for urgent support. Always read achievement (SS, PR) **and** growth (SGP) together, and bring questions to the teacher.
